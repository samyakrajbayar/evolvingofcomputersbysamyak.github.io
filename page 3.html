FIRST GENERATION COMPUTERS (1946-1958)


The First Generation of Computers started in 1946. Its makers used thousands of tubes, which were often huge, taking up the space of the entire room.
They were very expensive, used huge amount of electricity, and generated a lot of heat.
The first generation computers used Machine language, which was the lowest level programming language understood by the computer.


Features of First Generation Computers:


  • The size was very big
  • The operating speed was very slow
  • They were very expensive They used the device called vacuum tubes.
  • ENIAC and UNIVAC are the examples of first generation computers


Drawbacks of First Generation Computers:


  • They produced a lot of heat.
  • They were not much reliable
  • They used Machine language

To overcome these drawbacks, the second generation computers
were developed

FOURTH GENERATION COMPUTERS
(1971 ONWARDS)


The Fourth Generation of Computers started in 1971, and they used Microprocessors.
The size of computer became very small as thousands of Integrated Circuits onto a single Silicon Chip.
These computers became more powerful and they could be linked together to go network, which led to the development of Internet.
Intel 4004, Macintosh, IBM 370 etc. are the examples of fourth generation computer.
The programming languages used are C C++, java etc The fourth generation computers are so small that they can fit on a small desk or on the palm of the hand.
in 1981, IBM introduced its first Personal Computer (PC) for the home users and in 1984, Apple introduced the Macintosh.


Features of Fourth Generation Computers:


  • They use microprocessor
  • They are multipurpose computers.
  • They are accurate and reliable
  • The operating speed is very fast
